What Do I Need To Do Before Taking My Blood Pressure With The Application?

Before you can take your blood pressure with the application, you must do the following:

  1. Start the application

  2. Do the following if you have used the application on this device before:

    1. Select your name from the user list

    2. Perform a frequency scan if you have changed or recharged the stethoscope batteries since the last frequency scan, or if you need to recalibrate according to the next step

    3. Recalibrate the application if you have not done so in 1-3 months, or if you have started taking, stopped taking, or changed the dose of a medication since you last recalibrated

  3. Do the following if you have NOT used the application on this device before:

    1. Add yourself to the user list

    2. Perform a frequency scan

    3. Follow the steps in “How Do I Perform the First Calibration?” to do the first calibration

  4. Follow the steps in “How Do I Prepare To Take My Blood Pressure With The Application?”

How Do I Start the My Blood Pressure Application?


To start the application, follow these steps:

  1. If you are using any accessibility features on your smartphone involving audio (such as a screen reader), follow the instructions in the “The Application Prompts Me To Connect A Splitter” section

  2. Touch your phone's Home button (a button or icon with a picture of a house, typically located at the bottom center of your phone)

  3. Open your phone's list of applications using the on-screen icon that looks like a grid in the bottom center of your phone

  4. If your Android device has an “Apps” tab and a “Widgets” tab, touch the “Apps” tab

  5. Swipe down or to the right until you see the “My Blood Pressure” icon

  6. Touch the “My Blood Pressure” icon

  7. In the “User Select” menu, touch your name if it appears

  8. If your name does NOT appear in the “User Select” menu, see the “How Do I Add Myself To The List Of Users?” section below

    [top]

How Do I Add Myself To the List Of Users?

  1. Follow steps 1-5 of “How Do I Start the My Blood Pressure Application?” above.

  2. In the “User Select” menu, touch “New User”

  3. Touch the text box below “New User Name:”

  4. Type in your name

  5. Press OK

  6. Follow the instructions in “How Do I Perform A Frequency Scan?” to do the frequency scan

  7. Follow the instructions in “How Do I Perform the First Calibration?” to calibrate the application

    [top]

How Do I Perform a Frequency Scan?

Before the application can take your blood pressure, it has to know at what frequencies it can hear your heart. You can have the application do a frequency scan to find out the ideal frequencies. To do this, follow these steps:

  1. Follow the steps in “How Do I Start The My Blood Pressure Application?” section to start the application and select your user name

  2. Open the application's menu by touching either the Menu button or the icon that looks like three dots

  3. Touch “Frequency Scan”

  4. Follow the instructions in “How Should I Position Myself When Taking My Blood Pressure?”

  5. Press START

  6. Do NOT move until the application prompts you to press CONTINUE

You should redo the frequency scan before calibrating and when you charge or change batteries in the stethoscope.

How Do I Get the Stethoscope Attachment?

At present, the stethoscope microphone is not sold in stores. To make your own stethoscope microphone, you will need the following:

  • a stethoscope

  • a battery-powered microphone (unidrectional if possible) that meets the following requirements:

    • fits into one of the stethoscope's earpieces (you can widen one side of the earpiece's hole to make it fit)

    • connects to a 3.5mm audio jack

  • a pair of scissors

  • batteries for the microphone

  • a tool to widen a hole of one of the stethoscope's earpieces

To make your stethoscope microphone, follow these steps:

  1. Cut the stethoscope tubing approximately 4” (10cm) away from the diaphragm (the disk-shaped part of the stethoscope you would place on a person's heart)

  2. Remove one of the stethoscope's earpieces

  3. Find the side of the earpiece's hole that you could insert the stethoscope tubing into

  4. Widen the other side of the earpiece's hole as needed until the microphone fits tightly inside

  5. Insert the stethoscope tubing connected to the diaphragm into the side of the earpiece's hole you found in step 3

  6. Insert the microphone to the side of the earpiece's hole you widened in step 3

  7. Insert the batteries into the microphone's battery compartment according to the manufacturer's instructions

How Do I Prepare To Take My Blood Pressure With The Application?

To prepare to take your blood pressure with the application, do the following:

  1. Find an air conditioned room with a chair next to a table or other flat surface that is roughly level with your heart after you sit down in the chair

  2. Wear only one or two layers of clothing if possible

  3. Sit down in the chair

  4. Rest the arm you wish to use straight out on the flat surface

  5. Put your feet flat on the floor

  6. Do not cross your legs or ankles

  7. Cover the phone's camera and flash with the index finger on the extended arm

  8. Make sure the index finger touches the camera and flash

  9. Connect the stethoscope attachment to the smartphone

  10. Check the stethoscope's battery

  11. Turn the stethoscope on

    [top]

Where And How Do I Place the Stethoscope Attachment?

Find the imaginary line between your nipples. Use the hand you do not have extended on the table to hold the flat part of the stethoscope attachment on the line you found in step 10, in between the center of the chest and your left nipple. You may need to experiment with different locations on your heart to find where the stethoscope can hear your heart (not your lungs) best.

How Do I Take My Blood Pressure?

You will need the following:

  • an Android smartphone with the following:

    • a camera with flash

    • a charged battery

    • the My Blood Pressure application installed

  • a stethoscope attachment for your smartphone's audio jack (see the “How Do I Get the Stethoscope Attachment?” section for more information)

  • an air-conditioned room

  • a chair in the air-conditioned room

  • a hard surface on which you can rest your arm at heart level next to the chair

To take your blood pressure, follow these steps:

  1. Follow the steps in the “How Do I Start the My Blood Pressure Application?” section

  2. Follow the steps in the “How Do I Prepare To Take My Blood Pressure With The Application?” section

  3. Press the START button

  4. Follow the steps in the “Where And How Do I Place the Stethoscope Attachment?” section

  5. Remain still and do not talk until the app has finished reading your blood pressure

How Do I Calibrate the Application?

How Do I Use a Reading to Calibrate the Application?

  1. Take your blood pressure using the application (see the “How Do I Take My Blood Pressure?” section)

  2. At the “Do you want to calibrate the device using this reading?” prompt, touch “Yes”

  3. Touch the text box below the “Expected Systolic:” label

  4. Enter your expected systolic (the top number) pressure if you know it

  5. Touch the text box below the “Expected Diastolic:” label

  6. Enter your expected diastolic (the bottom number) pressure if you know it

  7. Touch the text box below the “Notes:” label

  8. Enter any notes you have for the run

  9. Touch “OK”

    [top]

How Do I Perform the First Calibration?

For best results, calibrate the application before using it. To calibrate the device, you will need the following:

  • an Android smartphone with the following:

    • a camera with flash

    • a charged battery

    • the My Blood Pressure application installed

  • a stethoscope attachment for your smartphone's audio jack (see the “How Do I Get the Stethoscope Attachment?” section for more information)

  • a blood pressure cuff

  • an air-conditioned room

  • a chair in the air-conditioned room

  • a hard surface on which you can rest your arm at heart level next to the chair

To calibrate the device, follow these steps:

  1. Remain relaxed and do not exercise, consume caffeine, smoke, or do anything else that might raise your blood pressure for at least 1-2 hours

  2. Follow the cuff manufacturer's instructions to take your blood pressure

  3. Remove the cuff

  4. Follow the steps in “How Do I Use A Reading to Calibrate the Application?”

  5. For 10-15 minutes, walk or jog as fast as safely possible

  6. Repeat steps 2-4 until your blood pressure stops decreasing

Ideally, you should take a total of at least 8 readings with the application during this process (including the reading immediately after step 1).

    [top]

How Often Do I Need To Calibrate the Application?

After the first calibration, you will need to recalibrate the application once every 1-3 months, depending on your health. Each time you start taking, stop taking, or change the dose of a medication, you should recalibrate the application.

How Do I Log My Blood Pressure?

  1. Take your blood pressure using the application (see the “How Do I Take My Blood Pressure?” section)

  2. At the “Would you like to calibrate the device using this reading?” prompt, touch “Log”.

  3. Touch the text box below the “Expected Systolic:” label

  4. Enter your expected systolic (the top number) pressure if you know it

  5. Touch the text box below the “Expected Diastolic:” label

  6. Enter your expected diastolic (the bottom number) pressure if you know it

  7. Touch the text box below the “Notes:” label

  8. Enter any notes you have for the run

  9. Touch “OK”

Troubleshooting

Why is My Finger Hot?

The application uses your camera's flash as a light source. If used for too long, the flash may get hot. Let the camera cool down for about an hour. Then, review the instructions in “How Can I Get My Blood Pressure Faster?”

    [top]

How Can I Get My Blood Pressure Faster?

To get your blood pressure faster, you need a better signal. To get a better signal, follow these steps:

  1. Review the instructions in “How Should I Position Myself When Taking My Blood Pressure?”

  2. Change the stethoscope attachment's battery

  3. Perform another frequency scan (see the “How Do I Perform A Frequency Scan?” section)


    [top]

Why is the Camera Not Turning On?

When an application needs one of your phone's resources, such as its camera(s), the application will borrow the resource from the Android operating system. When the application is finished using the resource, it will usually return the resource back to the Android operating system so that other applications can use the resource. In some cases, such as when a person “force stops” an application, the application does not properly return the resources it borrowed. If this happens, restart your phone.

    [top]

The Reading is not Accurate

  1. Review the instructions in “How Do I Prepare To Take My Blood Pressure?”

  2. Change the stethoscope attachment's battery

  3. Perform another frequency scan

  4. Read the “How Do I Calibrate the Application?” chapter

    [top]

The Application Prompts Me To Connect A Splitter

When you connect the stethoscope microphone directly to the smartphone, the smartphone may try to send all audio output through the audio jack, as if you connected headphones to it. If you need audio output when using the application, you will need to get headphones and an audio splitter that allows you to connect headphones and the stethoscope microphone to the smartphone's audio jack at the same time. Connect the splitter to the smartphone's audio jack according to the manufacturer's instructions. Then, connect the headphones and the stethoscope microphone to the splitter according to the manufacturer's instructions.
